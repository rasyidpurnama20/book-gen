# Comprehensive Indonesian Polymer Chemistry Textbook in LaTeX

A 1752-line single-file LaTeX textbook (Kimia Polimer Organik) covering organic polymer chemistry for Indonesian undergraduate students. The document delivers substantial scientific content across five chapters plus six appendices, with pedagogical elements (learning outcomes, exercises, discussion questions) in each chapter. All 89 LaTeX environments are properly balanced and the document will compile without structural errors.

**Watch for:** Front matter chapter titles and learning outcomes do not match actual chapter content (confirmed). The `\listoffigures` command is present but no figures exist in the document (confirmed). Dispersity symbol usage (`\DJ` and `\DH`) is inconsistent and produces wrong glyphs (confirmed). `\backmatter` before `\appendix` will suppress appendix letter-numbering (confirmed).

## High-level view

The front matter (Capaian Pembelajaran and Peta Konsep) describes a four-chapter organization with different titles and scope from what the actual chapters deliver. The learning outcomes reference "Bab 2: Struktur dan Sifat Polimer" and "Bab 4: Karakterisasi, Aplikasi, dan Polimer Berkelanjutan," but the actual chapters are "Jenis-Jenis Polimer Organik" and "Modifikasi Polimer dan Material Maju Polimer." Students navigating between the front matter and body will encounter significant confusion.

The document includes `\listoffigures` in the front matter but contains zero figure environments. This produces an empty page. For a chemistry textbook at this level, the complete absence of figures (mechanism diagrams, DSC curves, morphology illustrations) is a pedagogical gap.

The bibliography lists 31 references but zero `\cite` commands appear anywhere in the body. The `natbib` package is loaded but unused. Claims about production volumes, historical dates, and material properties cannot be traced to sources.

The `\backmatter`/`\appendix` ordering means the six appendix chapters will appear unnumbered rather than as "Lampiran A, B, C..." in the table of contents and headers.

## Front matter vs. body structural inconsistency

The "Capaian Pembelajaran Buku" (line 83) defines learning outcomes for:

- Bab 2: "Struktur dan Sifat Polimer" — expects taktisitas, kristalinitas, $T_g$/$T_m$, morphology
- Bab 3: "Polimerisasi dan Mekanismenya" — expects coordination polymerization section
- Bab 4: "Karakterisasi, Aplikasi, dan Polimer Berkelanjutan" — expects characterization methods, biodegradable polymers, circular economy

The actual chapters deliver:

- Chapter 2: "Jenis-Jenis Polimer Organik" — catalog of natural (cellulose, starch, chitin, chitosan) and synthetic polymers (PE, PP, PVC, PS, PET, rubber, aramid, nylon)
- Chapter 3: "Proses Polimerisasi dan Sintesis Polimer" — cationic, anionic, free radical, and condensation polymerization plus synthesis techniques and characterization methods
- Chapter 4: "Modifikasi Polimer dan Material Maju Polimer" — coatings, desalination membranes, batteries/supercapacitors, conductive polymers, smart polymers

A student expecting $T_g$ and morphology coverage from the learning outcomes for "Bab 2" will not find it there; those topics appear partially in Chapter 3's characterization section (DSC) and Chapter 4. The learning outcome "Menjelaskan sifat termal polimer termasuk suhu transisi gelas ($T_g$) dan suhu leleh ($T_m$)" has no corresponding section in Chapter 2. The "Peta Konsep" (line 127) compounds the problem by narrating the same phantom chapter structure.

## Empty \listoffigures

The `\listoffigures` command on line 145 will generate an empty page because the document contains zero figure environments or `\includegraphics` commands. Polymer structures, reaction mechanism diagrams, DSC/TGA thermograms, GPC chromatograms, membrane cross-section schematics, and polymer morphology illustrations are all absent. The `\ce{}` chemical equations partially substitute for structural formulas but cannot represent conformational or morphological information.

## Dispersity symbol rendering

Three occurrences use two different commands:

- Line 182: `\DJ` — produces Đ (D-with-stroke)
- Lines 789, 1729: `\DH` — produces Ð (Icelandic eth)

These are visually distinct characters. A single `\newcommand{\Dstroke}{\DJ}` in the preamble, used consistently, would resolve both the inconsistency and the readability issue.

## Bibliography without citations

The preamble loads `\usepackage[round]{natbib}` (line 23) but the document uses `\begin{thebibliography}{99}` (line 1264) and contains zero `\cite`, `\citet`, or `\citep` commands. The 31 references — including primary sources like Staudinger (1920), Ziegler (1955), and Shirakawa (1977) — are never connected to the claims they support. For a textbook that states specific dates, production volumes ("lebih dari 400 juta ton per tahun"), and material property values, the absence of in-text citations undermines academic rigor.

## `\backmatter` / `\appendix` ordering

Line 1258 declares `\backmatter` (suppresses chapter numbering), then line 1334 declares `\appendix` (attempts to switch to A, B, C numbering). Because `\backmatter` has already suppressed numbering, the appendix chapters will appear as unnumbered in the TOC and headers. The fix: place `\appendix` *before* `\backmatter`, or restructure so appendices sit between `\mainmatter` and `\backmatter`.

## Missing coordination polymerization content

Capaian Pembelajaran Bab 3 item 4: "Menjelaskan prinsip polimerisasi koordinasi menggunakan katalis Ziegler-Natta dan metalosen." Chapter 3 has subsections for cationic (line 748), anionic (line 771), and free radical (line 798) polymerization, but no corresponding subsection for coordination polymerization. Ziegler-Natta catalysts are mentioned in the Chapter 1 history section and when describing HDPE/PP production, but the catalytic mechanism, stereochemical control (isotactic vs. syndiotactic selectivity), active site models, and metallocene single-site catalysts are not treated as a standalone topic despite being promised in the learning outcomes.

## Minor LaTeX issues

The bibliography entry `\"Uber` (line 1280) should be `\"{U}ber` — without braces, some engines may interpret the `U` as part of a different command. Similarly `Poly\"athylen` should be `Poly\"{a}thylen`. With T1 encoding loaded, these will likely render correctly, but the braced form is the defensive standard.
